Travels

There's something great about road trips! With friends or family, they're truly a chance to disconnect from everything and focus on you destination ahead. I personally love going on road trips with friends and family. I look forward to the drive just as much my plans at my actual destination. Check out some of the places I've taken a trip to (pre-COVID). For all of them, my starting point was Chicago.

NEW YORK

There's simply so much to do and see in NY! I took a road trip there with my family during the summer of 2019, right before the start of my senior year of college. I think I've been to every tourist spot imagineable in the seven days I spent there. From the Statue of Liberty to the Empire State Building to taking the tram to Roosevelt Island. It's safe to say that NY is not driving friendly and I'm glad we figured that out right away so we didn't waste time driving. Trying NY style pizza was from Joe's was fun (even though Chicago tavern-style still takes the win for me!). My favorite part was visiting The MET and getting to see so many Van Gogh and Monet paintings less than two feet away from me. Overall, it was a really great experience.

WASHINGTON D.C.

Along with my trip to NYC, we also took a detour to DC! I was very pleasantly surprised by DC and I really love that city now. Most of my time was spent exploring the different museums and visiting different monuments. I also had some of the best food there, espcially at a restaurant named Sakina. The city was really great and thanks to this trip, I think I can actually see myself living there one day.

DC is a great city to explore and I really look forward to being there again! There's a lot of history there and the museums are all free! As a museum lover, there's never-ending exhibitions to explore across all the different museums. Although things closing at 5:00pm is a bit of bummer, the city offers a lot for all interests.

SAN DIEGO

So maybe this one's cheating, but it wasn't a road trip. But still, a truly memorable trip! I took this trip thanks to the Tapia Diversity in Tech conference that students from my school were funded to go to. So basically a free trip to California to not only explore San Diego, but also attend one of the largest tech conferences around. The conference was amazing. It was actually where I discovered U-M's School of Information and the MSI program. I gained a lot of confidence in my professional skills and had an amazing time with my fellow freinds that were on that trip. The food in San Diego is simply some of the best I've ever had. The fresh seafood is unmatched. San Diego was a great place to vist and roam around.

San Diego was a great place to visit. It's so vibrant and the weather was beautiful during my time there. The food was honestly some of the best I've had and I look forward to experiencing that again one day. I don't think San Diego is really a place I see myself living in, but it is a place I hope to revisit soon post-COVID.

SAINT LOUIS

Saint Louis was a spur of the moment I took with my friends to cheer on our school basketball team in Saint Louis. The Ramblers were poised to win Arch Madness and possbily secure another ticket to the Big Dance and we wanted to be there for it. Unfortunaly we lost right before the finals, but that didn't mean we didn't take advantage of our time there. The city had so many unexpected surprises to offer, especially the food. I got to try the original Saint Louis Bread Company (aka Panera) and a bunch of other restuarants that were simply amazing. St. Louis has a lot of halal options easily available, which I also appreciated. I went to the see the arch and also got a chance to check out a local dinner that had pretty great milkshakes.
